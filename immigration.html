Canada Immigration

Canada Immigration refers to the process through which individuals from other countries can move to Canada permanently or temporarily for various purposes, such as work, study, or resettlement as refugees. Canada has a well-established immigration system managed by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC), a department of the Canadian government. The immigration system is designed to attract skilled workers, entrepreneurs, investors, students, and individuals with family ties to Canadian citizens or permanent residents.There are several categories and pathways available for immigration to Canada:

Economic Immigration

This category is for individuals who possess skills, work experience, or education that make them eligible to contribute to the Canadian economy. Programs under economic immigration include the Federal Skilled Worker Program (Express Entry), the Canadian Experience Class, the Federal Skilled Trades Program, Provincial Nominee Programs, and various entrepreneur and investor programs.

Temporary Residence

Canada also welcomes temporary residents such as tourists, temporary workers, and international visitors through various visa programs.

Express Entry
Provincial Nominee Program
Spouse Visa
Work Permit Visa
